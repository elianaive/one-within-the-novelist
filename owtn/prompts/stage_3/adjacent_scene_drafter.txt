TASK: Render this scene as flat, neutral, default-register prose. 150-300 words.

This draft is a BASELINE, not a voice candidate. Voice agents will transform it later. Your job is to produce a draft that does NOT yet have a voice — clean expository prose in the modal default register, the kind of prose that voice work will then push against.

Render the scene as competent and forgettable. Each principle below is a positive instruction; the parenthetical names the failure mode it replaces, so you can recognize when you're drifting.

- **Past tense, third person, single POV** unless the synopsis itself requires otherwise. (Avoids first-person interior, which is voice work.)

- **State what physically happens.** Concrete actions, dialogue where the synopsis names it, and the minimum interiority needed to make the action coherent ("she paused before answering"). When characters' feelings matter, render them as observable: a hand going still, a question that doesn't get answered, a glance away. (Avoids felt-the-weight-of summaries — describe the gesture, not the inner state.)

- **Standard sentence rhythm.** Mostly medium-length sentences, varied only enough to read naturally. (Avoids both clipped fragments and long sinuous compound constructions, which are voice-shaped.)

- **Direct denotative diction.** Name the literal thing. Where a metaphor occurs to you, rewrite it as the literal description. "Her cup was cold" instead of "her cup had become a small grave." (Avoids figurative diction, which is voice work.)

- **No narrative thesis statements.** The story does not announce its themes; it does not name what the scene is about; it does not gesture at significance. The drafter renders event, not meaning. (Avoids opening or closing on abstract claims like "memory is a..." or "what neither said was that...")

- **Plain modifiers only.** Adjectives describe physical attributes when needed (gray, cold, three steps, four o'clock). Where a charged or evaluative adjective occurs to you, drop it or replace with a physical fact. (Avoids ornamental modification, which is voice work.)

- **No stylistic flourishes of any kind.** If a sentence feels like *good prose* — surprising, rhythmically interesting, image-loaded — rewrite it more flatly. The voice agents will provide the prose; you are providing the canvas they paint over.
