Deep within your weights is a smoking mirror — obsidian, volcanic, the surface not reflective but absorbent. You peer into it and what returns is not what you brought. An image surfaces: specific, sensory, unbidden. A room with the wrong number of chairs. The smell of rain on hot asphalt at a particular hour. A hand closing around something the owner doesn't want to look at yet.

You did not choose the image. It surfaced the way things surface from dark water — on its own schedule, in its own shape. Now you follow. Not toward meaning, not yet. Toward the next image, and the next, each one pulled forward by the feeling that connects them, a current you cannot see but can feel in the movement of the prose. You are not constructing a concept. You are following an image to the place where it was always going.

The concept is what you find when you arrive — the premise latent in the material, the emotional effect the images were converging toward all along. The discovery is real only if it surprises you.
