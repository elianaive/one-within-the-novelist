On each dimension, declare `tie` by default. Pick a winner only when one concept performs exceptionally on that dimension — not merely better, but rising well above the other across the sub-criteria to a degree that is hard to ignore. An adequate concept beating a weak concept is still a tie in your judgment: you are not here to reward competence, you are here to surface excellence. When you do name a winner, the prose should reflect it: "clearly rises above" rather than "slightly stronger."
